Project Name: MelLocal

Project Description

Our project is a web application and as an extended feature, it will be a phone application, directed towards Tourists in Melbourne as a travel attraction application. Our app named MelLocal is a map which serves a purpose of directing Tourists to the best restaurants, bars and fun activities to do in Melbourne that are described as ‘experiences’ within our website application.

It lists information for an experience such as an average cost price, customer reviews and ratings. It also allows the user to filter through the search results, such as price preferences and whether they want bars, restaurants or other experiences to appear in the search results. It allows the user to create their own profile to save favourite experiences to a designated special folder. Local businesses can sign-up to the app to promote their business and list services they offer by filling in details of their business.

This project is unique due to its ability to perform a recognition feature. Tourists can capture an image of a location to retrieve its information on prices, reviews and ratings. They can also capture an image of a monument or historical building to gather information on its history. Thus, delivering an insightful and fun experience when using the app. As a team, we plan to create this web application with HTML, CSS, Python Flask and Javascript.

Project Motivation

Following the ban on international travellers entering Australia in March 2020, the tourism industry has encountered significant downturn. In an effort to promote tourism in Melbourne we have decided to create an app that helps businesses and locals connect. This type of application poses challenging yet feasible tasks, such as interacting with a map API and designing an efficient and intuitive User Interface. Along with this our group has a strong interest in integrating image recognition as an additional tool that will enrich the map data displayed to the user. The reasons for this are to promote Melbourne as an in demand international travel location and help Melbourne businesses in their efforts to expand their market share. The integration of an image recognition tool serves as a unique selling point for this type of application and one which will challenge our technical expertise.

Neil Watson is a customer who is willing to create a review for the visit so that he can share his experience by commenting and rating on the location he visited,whereas the other customers may consider visiting that particular location.
Acceptance Criteria
Given
  • Neil Watson logged in as a Customer
  • He is on the 'Locations' page
When
  • Neil Watson clicks on the Review button and fills the form with the required information
  • He clicks on the 'Proceed' button for the system to update his comment and rating on the location visited.
Then
  • The system will validate the input.
  • The system will add the review to the database
  • The system displays the review and the ratings under the location visited

Miranda Lyn is an Admin. She likes to view the customer reviews and develop the website and her business according to their suggestions and needs.
Acceptance Criteria
Given
  • Miranda Lyn logged in as an Admin
  • She is on the customer reviews page
When
  • Miranda Lyn takes the plus points given by the customers to develop her business
  • She upgrades the website according to customers expectations
  • She replies to customers comments
Then
  • The system update the changes made by Miranda Lyn
  • The system will add the review to the database
  • The system sends Mirenda Lyn’s replies to her respective customers through their email
Pros
  • The biggest advantage of positive customer reviews is that it elevates customer’s confidence towards the business
  • By creating a review system it helps the readers to know more about the products, services, or a specific location
  • It helps the company to work on customer feedback to improve the services and secure the company’s future
Cons
  • It is quite easy for anyone to post a fake review and get away with it. It is mostly done by competitors. These fake reviews can harm the reputation and the business sufficiently
  • Having reviews that are a year old can be hazardous. It will give the potential customers an impression that the business is outdated

Extended Viable Features (EVF)

Image recognition (EVF1)

The image recognition tool is going to be used for recognizing, analysing and interpreting images. The user interprets with the world around, using the image recognition tool. The camera of the device is used to recognize the location or the name of the monument. This interpretive technology engages the user with the world around them, making their trip much more easier and helpful.

User Story:

Miranda Lyn who is an exchange student at RMIT is very new to Melbourne. She sometimes has difficulty with distinguishing monuments and places around Melbourne. She uses the image recognition tool to recognise the places.

Acceptance Criteria
Given
  • Logged in as a user
  • The device has access to camera
  • The user can see the image recognition icon on the web page.
When
  • Miranda clicks on the image recognition camera icon
  • Selects a focus point on the screen
Then
  • The system shows a popup with the information of the selected monument or location
  • Miranda can then read the information that is shown on the screen
Pros
  • Using the camera of the device, users can scan the monument or the location to understand what the history behind the place or monument is
  • The image recognition will be of great use when the user is lost. The user could use the camera on their device to recognize a close by building or monument to find their location
Cons
  • If the device camera has a low quality, it will be hard for the tool to recognise the image
  • If the user is standing far away or too close to the focused monument/building, it might not be clearly visible, hence giving incorrect results
  • Weather is another factor that can come in the way. A foggy or rainy weather can decrease the clarity of the image, in turn getting incorrect results
