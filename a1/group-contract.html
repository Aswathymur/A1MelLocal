Group Contract

This contract constitutes an agreement between all group members:

Expectations

  • Group members should have notifications for the Teams channel switched to ‘All Activity’ and should aim to respond to queries within a 24-48 hour time period
  • Members should aim to research the tools that will be required to complete the tasks for the project at least 1 week before beginning work on those tasks (i.e. learning how to branch from the master repository using Github, or if using a front end tool like Figma/Invision - research how that tool works and the fundamentals of creating deliverables using it )
  • Groups meetings should occur at least a weekly frequency and the duration should be 1-2 hours
  • Members should aim to keep each other informed of the progress of their tasks, or seek help if they are stuck on a particular part (other members may be able to resolve their issues)
  • Collaboration tools such as Google Drive and Trello and Github should be updated weekly so that there is no double up of work, and so that there isn’t an introduction of tools or platforms that are outside the scope of this project

Method of Peer Feedback

When it comes to complex problem solving, we aim to pair our group members into groups of two. This will assist in allowing one member to observe the problem solving process and gives the opportunity to ask questions and share knowledge.

During the agile stand up meetings, group members can have transparency over the amount of work that each of the other members is contributing to the project. This can also serve as a point in time in which we can reassign tasks to members who have extra time to spare. Reallocating resources in this manner can also take the load off members who may feel overwhelmed by the number of tasks they are expected to complete.
